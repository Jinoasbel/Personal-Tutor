## Spanish PM Warns Trump on Iran, Accuses US of Playing "Russian Roulette" with Millions of Lives

**Introduction**
In a stark rebuke that has amplified tensions between two NATO allies, Spanish Prime Minister Pedro Sanchez has doubled down on his criticism of the recent US and Israeli attack on Iran. Sanchez issued a grave warning, stating that the conflict risked playing "Russian roulette" with the lives of millions. His forceful stance comes in response to US President Donald Trump's threat to cut trade with Madrid over Spain's position, highlighting a significant rift within the Western alliance regarding military intervention in the Middle East.

**Sanchez Condemns "Reckless and Illegal" Actions**
Prime Minister Sanchez wasted no time in describing the bombing of Iran as "reckless and illegal," signalling a profound disagreement with the actions taken by the United States and Israel. Demonstrating his resolve, he has also taken concrete steps by barring US aircraft involved in the operation from utilizing Spanish naval and air bases.

In a powerful statement, Sanchez articulated the gravity of the situation: "We should learn from history and we can't play Russian roulette with the destiny of millions of people. The powers involved in this conflict should immediately stop the hostilities and rely on dialogue and diplomacy." He further emphasized the need for international coherence, urging nations to "act in a coherent way, defending the same values that we defend when we talk about Ukraine, Gaza, Venezuela, and Greenland." This underscores Spain's commitment to a consistent foreign policy based on shared values, regardless of the geographical location of the conflict.

**Trump's Threats and Underlying Discord**
The escalating diplomatic exchange comes amidst US President Donald Trump's threat to impose trade restrictions on Spain. This is not an isolated incident but rather an exacerbation of pre-existing friction between the two leaders. As reported, there is "no love lost" between President Trump and Prime Minister Sanchez, with both having been at odds previously. Trump has notably criticized Sanchez for failing to meet what he perceives as Spain's NATO obligations, specifically its contribution of 5% towards defense spending. This underlying discord had already "set the stage for very negative response and relationship between both sides," making the current clash over Iran a predictable, albeit concerning, development.

**A Leader Unwilling to Back Down**
Despite the pressure from the US, Pedro Sanchez has proven to be "not a leader to back down from a fry." He has consistently maintained that his objective is to uphold international order, adamantly stating that "you can't just commit one illegality in the place of another." Sanchez underlined that this principled position is one Spain would adopt "regardless of the conflict, whether it was in Ukraine or or Gaza," demonstrating a firm commitment to international law and ethical consistency.

Furthermore, the Spanish Prime Minister drew upon historical memory, recalling that the "Iraq war in 2003 is still very much fresh in the memory of the country" and its far-reaching international repercussions. He explicitly warned that the current situation "really could cause a great disaster," invoking the specter of past military interventions and their devastating consequences.

**Call for Diplomacy and Non-Combat Support**
In light of these dire warnings and historical lessons, Pedro Sanchez is steadfast in his call for a return to diplomatic means, deeming it the "most urgent" path forward. While Spain would "apply and certainly send out its support to its allies in the region," he made it unequivocally clear that this support "certainly not when it comes to any combat missions." This highlights Spain's readiness to engage in humanitarian or logistical support, but a firm refusal to participate in military combat, underscoring its commitment to peaceful resolution and de-escalation.

**Key Takeaways**
*   Spanish Prime Minister Pedro Sanchez has issued a strong condemnation of the US and Israeli attack on Iran, likening it to playing "Russian roulette" with millions of lives.
*   Sanchez has taken concrete action by barring US aircraft involved in the operation from using Spanish bases.
*   The condemnation has further strained relations between Spain and the US, following President Trump's threats of trade cuts and prior criticism regarding Spain's NATO defense spending.
*   Sanchez emphasized the need for an immediate stop to hostilities, advocating for dialogue and diplomacy, and a consistent defense of international values.
*   Spain's stance is rooted in historical lessons, particularly the memory of the 2003 Iraq War, and a commitment to preventing a "great disaster."
*   While Spain will support its allies, it explicitly rules out participation in any combat missions.

**Conclusion**
Prime Minister Pedro Sanchez's outspoken criticism represents a significant voice of dissent from a European leader against US military actions in the Middle East. His warnings about playing "Russian roulette" with human lives underscore a profound concern for international stability and a principled stand against what he deems "reckless and illegal" interventions. The rising tensions between Madrid and Washington, exacerbated by Trump's trade threats, reveal deep fissures within the NATO alliance. Spain's firm commitment to dialogue, diplomacy, and a consistent application of international law, coupled with its refusal to participate in combat missions, sets a clear precedent for its foreign policy amidst global conflicts.
